# Daily Briefing — Saturday, 6 June 2026

## EU Affairs

### Main stories

**Brussels relaxes the 3% deficit rule to fund energy resilience — and hands Meloni a win.** The European Commission has agreed to loosen the bloc's 3%-of-GDP budget-deficit ceiling to let member states invest in energy security and resilience, a move [Euractiv](https://www.euractiv.com) frames explicitly as "a victory for…Giorgia Meloni", whose government has long pushed for more fiscal headroom to fund energy and industrial policy. The change forms part of a wider economic news round-up that also flags a record year for eurozone debt issuance (see Economy & Markets, below).

**The EU is reportedly considering scaling back protections for Ukrainian men of fighting age.** According to [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/eu-considers-limiting-protection-ukrainian-men-fighting-age-2026-06-04/), Brussels is weighing changes that would limit the temporary-protection status currently granted to Ukrainian men who would otherwise be eligible for military service — a sensitive shift that touches both EU asylum policy and Kyiv's manpower needs as the war with Russia continues. The story sits alongside reporting that Vladimir Putin has rejected a proposed meeting with Volodymyr Zelensky to end the war (see Foreign Policy).

**Brussels' tech-sovereignty push is "going soft" on Washington, Euractiv's editors argue.** In its "Get off of my cloud?" Editors' Choice newsletter, [Euractiv](https://www.euractiv.com) examines the EU's new tech-sovereignty package and the bloc's cloud-and-AI development authority (CAIDA), arguing that, in practice, the rules are being watered down to avoid antagonising major US cloud providers — a tension between the EU's stated ambition for digital independence and its commercial reliance on American technology firms.

## Italian Politics

### Main stories

**Italy's parliament moves to require parental consent for sex education, citing "gender propaganda".** [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/italy-introduces-parental-consent-sex-education-schools-2026-06-05/) reports that Italy has introduced a bill requiring schools to obtain parents' consent before pupils take part in sex-education lessons, with backers of the measure framing it as a defence against what they call "gender propaganda" in the classroom — a move that places Italy alongside a small group of European states tightening rules in this area (Poland's recent move to restrict pupils' access to pornography via a school phone ban, also reported by [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/media-telecom/poland-ban-phones-schools-restrict-access-pornography-2026-06-02/), offers a loose parallel, though the Italian measure is squarely about curriculum consent rather than device policy).

**Mario Seminerio takes apart Marco Fortis's claim that Italian youth unemployment is collapsing.** Responding to a Marco Fortis op-ed in *Il Messaggero* trumpeting a fall in Italy's youth unemployment rate from 20.3% in April 2025 to 16.9% in April 2026 — supposedly the steepest drop anywhere in the EU — [Mario Seminerio on phastidio.net](https://phastidio.net/2026/06/05/basta-qualche-ciliegia-e-la-disoccupazione-va-giu/) goes back to the underlying Istat figures and finds a less flattering picture: the number of unemployed 15-24-year-olds did fall by roughly 63,000 over the year, but that was accompanied by a jump of about 134,000 in the number of young people classified as economically "inactive" (pushing the inactivity rate up by 1.9 percentage points), while the number actually in work fell by some 40,000. Seminerio's conclusion — wry but pointed — is that the headline rate is improving largely because young Italians are leaving the labour market altogether, not because they are finding jobs.

## Foreign Policy

### Main stories

**The US has struck Iranian coastal sites after Tehran launched drones, in the latest Gulf escalation.** [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/us-attacks-iranian-coastal-sites-after-iran-launches-drones-latest-flare-up-2026-06-06/) reports that American forces attacked Iranian coastal radar and missile sites in response to a drone launch from Iran, the latest exchange in a conflict that continues to keep the Strait of Hormuz under severe strain. [Le Grand Continent](https://legrandcontinent.eu/fr/2026/06/05/vers-un-trafic-fantome-dans-le-detroit-dormuz/) reports that daily AIS-tracked shipping through the Gulf remains more than 97% below pre-war levels, that roughly two-thirds of laden tankers leaving the Gulf in May sailed with their identification systems switched off to evade Iranian surveillance, and that the US Central Command has quietly guided some 70 vessels through the strait over the past three weeks; floating oil storage in the Gulf has fallen by around 30 million barrels since March (from about 180 million to 150 million), while Brent crude — capped partly by Gulf states routing exports around the strait via pipelines in Saudi Arabia and the UAE — is trading at around $95 a barrel, having last topped $120 in late February. Tehran is reportedly discussing a permanent transit toll for the strait with Oman, while Washington insists traffic must remain unimpeded — a standoff that leaves the blockade, and the wider war, without an obvious end point.

**Donald Trump's "crazy" rebuke of Binyamin Netanyahu has landed at a delicate moment, as Israel presses ahead with a major West Bank settlement expansion.** [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/trumps-crazy-rebuke-undercuts-netanyahu-critical-moment-2026-06-05/) reports that the US president's blunt criticism of the Israeli prime minister has undercut him just as [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/israel-plans-big-west-bank-settlement-push-many-see-illegal-2026-06-03/) separately reports that Israel is planning a large new settlement push in the occupied West Bank that many in the international community regard as illegal under international law — a combination that is straining the US-Israel relationship even as fighting continues on multiple fronts.

**Putin has rejected a proposed meeting with Zelensky to end the war, and Kyiv's outreach appears aimed elsewhere.** [The Times](https://www.thetimes.com/world/russia-ukraine-war/article/putin-rejects-zelensky-meeting-end-russia-ukraine-war-20jbg6tfh) reports that the Russian president has turned down the prospect of direct talks with his Ukrainian counterpart, while [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/zelenskiys-letter-putin-was-intended-other-ears-2026-06-05/) suggests that a recent letter from Zelensky to Putin was less a genuine peace overture than a message crafted with other audiences — chiefly Washington and European capitals — in mind.
