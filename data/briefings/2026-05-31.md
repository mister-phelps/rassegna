## European Affairs

**EU Council reaches provisional deal on AI Liability Directive**
European Union member states reached a provisional agreement late Friday on the AI Liability Directive, resolving a months-long impasse between governments favouring lighter-touch rules and MEPs pushing for stronger protections for citizens harmed by algorithmic decisions. The compromise text introduces a rebuttable presumption of causality — meaning plaintiffs no longer need to prove exactly how an AI system caused their harm, only that it plausibly did — while carving out exemptions for low-risk and open-source models below a certain capability threshold. The Council and Parliament must now formally approve the text before it is published in the Official Journal. The directive is expected to enter into force in tandem with the AI Act's full application in August 2026. *[Euractiv](https://www.euractiv.com/section/artificial-intelligence/news/eu-council-ai-liability-directive-deal/)*

**Von der Leyen signals early reshuffle ahead of trade negotiations**
Commission President Ursula von der Leyen is considering reassigning the trade portfolio ahead of the autumn round of EU–US tariff negotiations, according to three officials familiar with the discussions. The move would reflect frustration within the Berlaymont over the pace of talks with Washington, where the Trump administration has maintained steel and aluminium tariffs despite a January truce. A reshuffle of this scope would require sign-off from the College of Commissioners and could trigger broader realignments in the executive's leadership structure. *[Politico Europe](https://www.politico.eu/article/von-der-leyen-trade-reshuffle-us-tariffs/)*

## European Economy

**Eurozone unemployment hits record low of 5.8% in April**
Eurostat data released Friday showed eurozone unemployment fell to 5.8% in April, the lowest level since the euro was introduced, as labour markets remained tight across most member states despite slowing growth. Germany was the notable outlier, with its unemployment rate edging up to 5.1% amid ongoing restructuring in the automotive and chemicals sectors. ECB chief economist Philip Lane said the figures reinforced the central bank's view that domestic price pressures, particularly in services, would remain elevated for longer than previously forecast, complicating the path to further rate cuts. *[Financial Times](https://www.ft.com/content/eurozone-unemployment-record-low/)*

## International Politics

**Ceasefire talks in Kyiv stall as Russia rejects neutrality guarantees**
A third round of indirect ceasefire talks between Ukrainian and Russian delegations, mediated by Turkey and the UAE in Ankara, ended without agreement after Moscow rejected a draft framework that would have provided Ukraine with security guarantees falling short of full NATO membership. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky said Kyiv would not accept any arrangement that left it without credible defence commitments, while the Kremlin insisted that any peace deal must include a permanent cap on the size of Ukraine's armed forces. Western officials described the gap between the two sides as "significant but not unbridgeable." *[Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/europe/ukraine-russia-ceasefire-ankara-talks/)*

**G7 foreign ministers agree framework for frozen asset use**
Meeting in Rome, G7 foreign ministers endorsed a legal framework that would allow interest accrued on approximately €260 billion in frozen Russian sovereign assets — held primarily at Euroclear in Belgium — to be disbursed directly to Ukraine for reconstruction purposes, without touching the underlying principal. The framework, which required months of negotiations over legal liability and precedent concerns, is expected to unlock around €3 billion annually. Russia condemned the decision as theft and threatened retaliatory measures against European financial institutions. *[The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/world/g7-foreign-ministers-frozen-assets-ukraine/)*

## Defence & Security

**NATO allies finalise enhanced forward presence in Romania and Bulgaria**
Alliance defence ministers meeting in Prague signed off on a significant expansion of NATO's Enhanced Forward Presence in the Black Sea region, with additional rotational battalions to be deployed to Romania and Bulgaria from autumn 2026. The move, backed unanimously by all 32 member states, is intended to signal resolve following Russian naval activity in the Black Sea and pressure on Moldova. Canada will lead the multinational battlegroup in Romania; France will anchor the Bulgarian deployment. *[Politico Europe](https://www.politico.eu/article/nato-enhanced-forward-presence-black-sea-romania-bulgaria/)*
