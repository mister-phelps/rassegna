# Daily Briefing — Wednesday, 3 June 2026

## European Affairs

**The assault on the modern state: neo-patrimonialism as the defining political contest**
Political scientists Stephen E. Hanson and Jeffrey S. Kopstein argue, in a major interview published today by [Le Grand Continent](https://legrandcontinent.eu/fr/2026/06/02/assaut-etat/), that the central political battle of our time is not simply between democracy and authoritarianism, but between the modern impersonal rule-of-law state and a return to neo-patrimonialism — a pre-modern form of power in which the state is treated as personal property of the ruler. They identify Trump, Putin, and Netanyahu as the key vectors of this shift, pointing to DOGE as an instrument not of efficiency but of reorientation: dismantling neutral bureaucracy to replace it with personal loyalty. The authors draw parallels with the Khodorkovski affair in Russia and note that Hungary under Orbán is the most advanced European case, while Nigel Farage's Reform UK is a live threat in Britain. Democrats, they warn, have not grasped that this is a contest over the nature of the regime itself, not a conventional left-right competition.

**Pope Leon XIV as Europe's paradoxical ally against Trump**
In an interview also published today by [Le Grand Continent](https://legrandcontinent.eu/fr/2026/06/02/prodi-encyclique/), former European Commission President Romano Prodi offers his first public reading of the papal encyclical *Magnifica Humanitas*. Prodi argues that Leon XIV — the first American pope — has become an unexpected champion of European values: the social market economy, regulated AI, multilateralism, and worker dignity. The encyclical is explicitly anti-imperialist and, in Prodi's reading, anti-Trump, drawing on the biblical opposition between Babel (concentrated power and pride) and Nehemiah's Jerusalem (cooperative rebuilding). Prodi describes the dynamic as neo-Guelph versus neo-Ghibelline: the "Ghibellines" are now at Donald Trump's court. He laments, however, that Europe has no political structure capable of translating the papal voice into action: "The Pope's voice is there, but what use is it if there is no political will to act on it?" On Italy specifically, Prodi is blunt — Meloni's pro-European repositioning is purely tactical, not a genuine political project.

## Economy & Markets

**Europe's solar boom is generating a structural grid crisis — and a Chinese storage dependency**
[Phastidio.net](https://phastidio.net/2026/06/02/come-evitare-di-scottarsi-col-solare/) publishes a detailed analysis of the contradictions in Europe's energy transition. With 490GW of solar capacity installed — more than gas, wind, nuclear, and coal combined — Europe faces a paradox of abundance: approximately 40 terawatt-hours of solar electricity will be curtailed this summer (up 25% on 2025), equivalent to Greater London's annual consumption. Hours of negative electricity prices have exploded — Spain logged 423 hours below zero between January and 11 May 2026, against 127 in the same period of 2024; Germany and France follow the same trajectory. In Spain, 16% of solar generation was cut in Q1 2026, double the 2024 rate. The root cause is the "cannibalisation effect": too much capacity producing simultaneously, with inadequate storage or grid flexibility to absorb it. Battery costs have fallen 90% since 2010, but over 1,700GW of renewable capacity awaits grid connection across 16 EU member states and Britain. A further risk: the dominant lithium-iron-phosphate battery chemistry is over 90% Chinese-controlled, meaning Europe risks swapping one energy dependency for another. The article also flags the regressive distributional effect: as variable costs fall, rising fixed system costs hit low-income households proportionally harder.

**Morocco as China's Trojan Horse in EU industrial policy**
[Phastidio.net](https://phastidio.net/2026/06/01/marocco-cavallo-di-troia-cinese-in-ue/) reports on EU concerns over Chinese firms using Morocco's free-trade access to the EU as a back-door for industrial exports. The Mohammed VI Tanger Tech City near Tangier hosts nearly a dozen Chinese auto-component manufacturers, while Gotion High-tech — a Chinese battery maker 25%-owned by Volkswagen — is building a $1.3bn gigafactory at Kenitra. Rhodium Group estimates Chinese greenfield investment in Morocco since the pandemic at roughly $6bn. EU Trade Commissioner Maroš Šefčovič has flagged "transshipment" risk, and the Commission has already acted twice — imposing countervailing duties on Moroccan fibreglass and aluminium rims with Chinese subsidy links. The EU's proposed Industrial Accelerator Act (tabled 4 March 2026), which aims to restore manufacturing's share of EU GDP from 14.3% to 20% by 2035, contains a critical loophole: firms producing in free-trade-agreement countries including Morocco could qualify for "EU-equivalent" status in public procurement. Brussels faces a dilemma: tightening rules risks collateral damage to Renault, Stellantis, and Volkswagen, all of which have significant Moroccan operations.

## Foreign Policy & Geopolitics

**Russian advance in Ukraine falls to near-zero in May**
According to [Le Grand Continent](https://legrandcontinent.eu/fr/2026/06/02/la-progression-russe-en-ukraine-est-desormais-quasi-nulle/), citing Ukrainian analytical group Deep State, Russia occupied just 14km² of additional Ukrainian territory in May 2026 — its lowest rate of advance since October 2023. This occurred despite a 37.5% surge in assault frequency, with over 7,000 Russian attacks recorded in May (approximately 225 per day). The Institute for the Study of War had estimated Russia lost 116km² of territory in April. Deep State notes that in practice Russia actually retreated last month, with some Ukrainian gains not published in real time for security reasons. The analysis identifies structural causes: drone saturation has extended the combat zone tens of kilometres deep, while Ukrainian strikes on fuel depots, ammunition stores, and command nodes degrade Russian logistics. Russian casualties remain approximately 1,100 per day — the same rate as a year ago — with no commensurate territorial gain.

**Syria allows Russia to resupply its Hmeimim airbase**
[Le Grand Continent](https://legrandcontinent.eu/fr/2026/06/02/face-a-lincertitude-du-soutien-de-trump-la-syrie-maintient-des-liens-militaires-avec-la-russie/) reports, citing satellite imagery and the *Wall Street Journal*, that Russia has resupplied its Hmeimim airbase in northwestern Syria for the first time since December 2024. The sanctioned cargo ship Sparta was loaded in St Petersburg, traversed the Mediterranean escorted by two Russian warships, and docked at Tartus in early May. Syrian leader Ahmed al-Charaa authorised the resumption of Russian military flights to Hmeimim in late October. Swedish defence analyst Aron Lund suggests Damascus sees Moscow as a more reliable security guarantor than the unpredictable Trump administration. Al-Charaa called Trump on 31 May to request the lifting of remaining US sanctions — Syria retains its designation as a state "sponsoring terrorism" — but US support "cannot be taken for granted," according to Lund.
