# Daily Briefing — Tuesday, 9 June 2026

## EU Affairs

**Ukraine's EU bid teeters between ambition and anxiety**
Ukraine's ambition to open multiple negotiating clusters with the EU before the end of summer is creating friction between Kyiv, Brussels and several European capitals ahead of the formal launch of the first cluster, expected on 15 June, according to diplomats cited by the [Politico](https://www.politico.eu) Brussels Playbook. Kyiv fears its membership bid could be "parked" due to France's upcoming presidential election and is alarmed by Franco-German discussion papers floating "associate membership" and other interim solutions. "In practice, Ukraine is being offered everything except membership itself," Ukraine's Ambassador to the EU Vsevolod Chentsov told Politico. Meanwhile, according to [Euractiv](https://www.euractiv.com), President Zelenskyy met US envoys Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner in Chișinău on Monday, focused on reviving diplomacy to end the war and discussing preparations for upcoming G7 meetings. [Le Grand Continent](https://legrandcontinent.eu/fr/2026/06/09/a-partir-de-demain-la-guerre-en-ukraine-sera-aussi-longue-que-la-premiere-guerre-mondiale/) notes that as of tomorrow (10 June), Russia's full-scale invasion will have lasted as long as the First World War — 1,567 days — while Ukraine has been under Russian aggression since 2014 for a total of 4,447 days, twice the duration of the Second World War.

**Franco-German FCAS fighter jet programme collapses after nine years**
France and Germany have officially abandoned their jointly planned next-generation fighter aircraft at the heart of the €100 billion Future Combat Air System (FCAS/SCAF), according to [Euractiv](https://www.euractiv.com), [Politico](https://www.politico.eu), and a detailed account by [Le Grand Continent](https://legrandcontinent.eu/fr/2026/06/08/paris-et-berlin-actent-labandon-de-leur-projet-commun-davion-de-combat-et-maintenant/). The breakdown follows years of industrial acrimony between Dassault Aviation, the French lead contractor, and Airbus Defence and Space, centred on questions of industrial leadership and the use of US ITAR-controlled components. A last-ditch mediation between the two companies failed in mid-April; Chancellor Merz had already cast doubt on the programme in February, citing the growing role of drones and incompatible French specifications — arguments perceived in Paris as a face-saving tactic. Per Le Grand Continent, the formal announcement is expected at the Berlin International Airshow (ILA, 10–14 June). Germany is now reportedly eyeing participation in the rival GCAP programme being developed by the UK, Japan and Italy. Both governments stress that the wider "system of systems" — a shared digital backbone linking aircraft, drones and other assets — will continue, as will the companion MGCS tank programme, though the latter has also been beset by industrial disputes.

**EU leaders likely to snub Trump's 4th of July party in Brussels**
The United States is hosting a large invite-only celebration of its 250th anniversary of independence for over 5,000 guests at Brussels' Autoworld Museum on 28 June, but as [Euractiv](https://www.euractiv.com) reports, the most senior EU figures are unlikely to attend. Commission President von der Leyen has said she will be travelling; Council President Costa's attendance remains "undecided"; and Kallas is unlikely to make it. Roberta Metsola, who has confirmed her attendance, is thus set to be the most senior EU representative. Belgian Prime Minister De Wever and NATO Secretary-General Rutte are expected to attend. US Ambassador Puzder's team expressed confidence that high-level attendees would be present; an embassy spokesperson declined to name them for "security reasons."

**Armenia's Pashinyan eyes EPP membership after pro-Western election victory**
Armenian Prime Minister Nikol Pashinyan, fresh from a commanding election victory on Sunday, is in talks to bring his pro-EU Civil Contract party into the European People's Party, [Euractiv](https://www.euractiv.com) has learned. No formal timetable has been set, but EPP approval could come later this year. [The Times](https://www.thetimes.co.uk) and [Le Grand Continent](https://legrandcontinent.eu/fr/2026/06/08/les-elections-armenie-marquent-une-perte-dinfluence-russe/) report that Russia issued stark warnings of "upheaval," with Putin having threatened that Armenia could face the same fate as Ukraine. Le Grand Continent frames the result as another setback for Moscow's strategy in its neighbourhood, arguing that Russia "is losing its empire" in the post-Soviet space. Other parties set to join the EPP include Hungary's Tisza, Denmark's Liberal Alliance and Montenegro's ruling Europe Now Movement.

**EU to take France and Spain to court over cybersecurity law**
The European Commission is preparing to launch infringement proceedings against France and Spain for failing to transpose the NIS2 cybersecurity directive into national law by the October 2024 deadline, according to the [Politico](https://www.politico.eu) Playbook.

## Italian Politics

**Italy's local elections deliver a dead heat**
Six provincial capitals voted in run-offs on Sunday, with the centre-left and centre-right each winning three, according to [Euractiv](https://www.euractiv.com). Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni hailed the result as evidence of her coalition's enduring local strength. Turnout fell to 52%, continuing a broader trend of voter disengagement.

**Opposition revives wealth-tax debate — but is it going anywhere?**
PD leader Elly Schlein has reignited Italy's perennial "patrimoniale" (wealth tax) debate, initially calling for a "European wealth tax" before rowing back in front of Confindustria's young industrialists, saying the measure is not part of her programme "yet." [Phastidio.net](https://phastidio.net/2026/06/08/teatrino-italia-commedia-patrimoniale-in-replica/) (Mario Seminerio) argues the debate is largely political theatre: coalition partner Giuseppe Conte has shown little enthusiasm, preferring his favoured "windfall profits" approach, and Schlein has already pivoted to a competing proposal — a three-year €200/month grant for under-35s, funded by a surtax on fast-growing companies — which Phastidio.net characterises as a penalty on productive, innovative firms rather than a genuine reform. Seminerio contends that a revenue-neutral shift of the tax burden from labour to capital could be useful, but that Italy's political class invariably turns such debates into additive tax rises, making any substantive reform "zero probability."

## UK Politics

**Starmer's children's social media ban faces legal onslaught from tech giants**
Ministers are braced for a wave of lawsuits from technology companies over Sir Keir Starmer's expected announcement next week of a ban on social media for children, broadly modelled on Australia's approach, according to [The Times](https://www.thetimes.co.uk). Planned carve-outs for platforms deemed "educational" are complicating the picture — industry sources say the categorisation decisions will invite legal challenges, with potential disputes over whether, for instance, TikTok and Instagram are banned whilst YouTube Kids is not. The Australian government itself faced a Reddit lawsuit over privacy and political-speech concerns when it implemented its own ban. The UK government said it was "determined to act quickly, but will do so in a way that is effective, enforceable and genuinely keeps children safe."

## Foreign Policy

**Israel and Iran trade blows for the first time since April ceasefire**
Israel struck a petrochemical plant in Iran's south-west and several military targets on Monday — the first hit on an Iranian energy installation since the April 8 ceasefire — as [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com) and the [Thomson Reuters Daily Briefing](https://www.reuters.com) report. The strike followed Iran's launch of missiles at Israel in retaliation for Israeli attacks on Lebanese targets over the weekend, itself carried out in defiance of pressure from President Trump, who has insisted he should control how Israel responds. [Politico](https://www.politico.eu) notes that there is a "growing strategic gulf between Israel and the US over the war in the Middle East." Iran blamed the United States for the latest exchanges. Oil prices climbed more than $4 on the news, major Gulf markets fell, and Iran's envoy to Moscow was quoted saying the Strait of Hormuz would remain open but with transit fees imposed.

**European leaders back Ukraine–Russia ceasefire talks**
The leaders of Britain, Germany and France said they support a proposal for direct talks between President Zelenskyy and President Putin to try to secure a ceasefire, with Europe playing a role, according to [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com). A spokesperson for Chancellor Merz separately said European leaders are "ready to assume the leading role" in such negotiations. Zelenskyy said he met US envoys Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner during a Chișinău stopover on Monday to discuss preparations for the G7 summit and related June meetings, per [Euractiv](https://www.euractiv.com).

**Xi visits Pyongyang as North Korean economy booms**
Chinese President Xi Jinping arrived in Pyongyang for a rare summit with Kim Jong Un, declaring that ties are at a "new historical starting point," as [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com) reports. The visit comes as [Le Grand Continent](https://legrandcontinent.eu/fr/2026/06/08/pourquoi-leconomie-nord-coreenne-connait-elle-un-boom-depuis-2023/) notes that North Korea's economy has been growing at its fastest rate in eight years since 2023, boosted by its deepened ties with Moscow and Beijing since the launch of Russia's full-scale invasion of Ukraine.
