# Daily Briefing — Wednesday, 1 June 2026

## European Affairs

**EU agrees new Common Return Regulation**
The European Parliament and the Council reached a political agreement on 1 June 2026 on the Regulation establishing a new Common European System for Returns. The regulation, presented by Home Affairs Commissioner Magnus Brunner, introduces harmonised procedures for issuing return decisions and a new “European Return Order” — ending the current patchwork of 27 divergent national systems. According to the [Commission's announcement](https://home-affairs.ec.europa.eu/news/commission-welcomes-political-agreement-return-regulation-2026-06-02_en), this forms a key pillar of the EU Migration Pact. Home affairs ministers are expected to formally endorse the deal this week alongside discussions on the Schengen area and the future legal status of displaced Ukrainians.

**Morocco as China's Trojan horse in the EU single market**
An investigation by the *Financial Times* — analysed in depth by [Phastidio.net](https://phastidio.net/2026/06/01/marocco-cavallo-di-troia-cinese-in-ue/) — documents how Chinese manufacturers are flooding Morocco with investment to exploit its free-trade agreement with the EU. The Mohammed VI Tanger Tech City near Tangiers hosts almost a dozen Chinese auto-component firms; battery maker Gotion High-tech (25% Volkswagen-owned) is building a €1.3 billion gigafactory at Kenitra. Rhodium Group estimates Chinese greenfield investment in Morocco since the pandemic at around $6 billion. EU Trade Commissioner Maros Sefcovic has denounced the risk of "transshipment" — Chinese overcapacity routed into Europe via preferential-tariff partners. The EU has previously imposed anti-dumping duties on Moroccan glass fibre and aluminium rims. As Phastidio notes, the proposed Industrial Accelerator Act (March 2026) contains a loophole: firms producing in free-trade-agreement countries may qualify for "Made in EU" equivalence in public procurement, potentially gutting its intent. Renault and Stellantis, with major Moroccan operations, complicate any aggressive EU response.

**Europe's solar energy boom hits a structural wall**
As analysed by [Phastidio.net](https://phastidio.net/2026/06/02/come-evitare-di-scottarsi-col-solare/) on 2 June, Europe's 490 GW of installed solar capacity is generating acute systemic dysfunction. Some 40 terawatt-hours will be wasted in summer 2026 through grid curtailment, up 25% on last year and equivalent to Greater London's annual consumption. In Spain, 16% of solar output was curtailed in Q1 2026 (double the 2025 rate); Germany reached 13%. Spain logged 423 negative-price electricity hours in the first four months of 2026 versus 127 in the same period of 2024. The crisis stems from a decade of subsidy-driven capacity build-out without commensurate grid and storage investment. Battery costs have fallen 90% since 2010, but EU storage projects face 1,700 GW of grid connection backlogs across 16 member states, and the dominant LFP battery chemistry remains over 90% dependent on Chinese supply chains.

**EU Foreign Affairs Committee votes on Ukraine and Moldova accession reports**
The European Parliament's Foreign Affairs Committee is voting this week on reports assessing the EU membership applications of Ukraine and Moldova. The Ukraine draft commends Kyiv's "extraordinary efforts to strengthen democratic institutions during wartime." Informal opening of all accession chapters began in March 2026, bypassing Hungary's veto. German Chancellor Friedrich Merz has pushed for an accelerated process with privileged single-market access. The European Council has committed a €90 billion loan to Ukraine for 2026–2027 (€60 billion military) and the EUMAM UA training mission has trained 75,000+ troops, per [Council documents](https://www.consilium.europa.eu/en/policies/eu-solidarity-ukraine/timeline-russia-military-aggression-against-ukraine/).

## Italian Politics

**Meloni holds firm after referendum defeat**
Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni continues to govern without sign of instability following her first major political setback — a 54% No vote in the March 2026 constitutional referendum on judicial reform. According to [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-09/italy-s-meloni-vows-to-govern-until-end-of-five-year-mandate) (9 April), she explicitly ruled out resignation or early elections and pledged to serve the full mandate through 2027. She addressed the annual Confindustria general meeting in Rome, where its president again called for accelerated EU fiscal integration — characterised by [Phastidio.net](https://phastidio.net/2026/05/26/europa-fai-presto-dacce-li-sordi/) as a push for transfer payments flowing predominantly toward Italy. On migration, Meloni wrote to EU Council and Commission presidents calling for enhanced coordination of border controls for arrivals from "affected areas."

## UK Politics

**Starmer survives — for now — as Labour crisis deepens**
Prime Minister Keir Starmer remains in office but faces severe internal pressure following catastrophic local election results in May 2026. Labour lost 1,498 councillors and control of 38 councils, including losses in London (Lambeth, Lewisham to the Greens) and northern England seats ceded to Reform UK. Health Secretary Wes Streeting resigned on 14 May, citing loss of confidence in Starmer's leadership. Starmer refused to resign, according to [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/5/8/uk-elections-early-results-and-takeaways-will-starmer-have-to-resign). Mayor of Greater Manchester Andy Burnham is positioning for a leadership challenge: Labour MP Josh Simons resigned his seat to trigger the Makerfield by-election, enabling Burnham's potential return to parliament. The [2026 Labour leadership crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Labour_Party_leadership_crisis) continues to unfold.

## Economy & Markets

**ECB expected to hike rates amid Middle East energy shock**
The ECB is expected to raise its main policy rate by 25 basis points in June to 2.25%, as policymakers judge that Middle East energy price pressures are no longer transitory. Brent crude is trading around $110/barrel and Dutch TTF gas around €50/MWh. Eurozone GDP growth decelerated from 0.3% in Q4 2025 to 0.2% in Q1 2026, while headline inflation is forecast to rise from 2.1% in 2025 to 2.7% in 2026. The unemployment rate edged up to 6.3% in April but remains near historic lows. According to [S&P Global Ratings](https://www.spglobal.com/ratings/en/regulatory/article/economic-research-economic-outlook-europe-q2-2026-global-shock-leaves-recovery-uncertain-s101675412), structural labour-market resilience should limit lasting damage.

## Foreign Policy & Geopolitics

**Ukraine war: EU deepens military commitment as ceasefire risks loom**
The EU has committed €90 billion in assistance to Ukraine for 2026–2027, with the EUMAM UA training mission extended to November 2026 having trained 75,000+ troops. The European Council on Foreign Relations, in its [2026 outlook](https://ecfr.eu/article/2026-the-year-we-stop-pretending-its-just-a-phase/), warns that a ceasefire on Russia's terms remains a top-tier risk that could reward aggression and leave Ukraine structurally vulnerable. Russia continues sub-threshold hybrid operations. The June 23 deadline for EU Russia sanctions renewal is approaching. According to [CSIS](https://www.csis.org/analysis/how-europe-can-build-ukraines-future-force), European states face the challenge of transitioning from reactive support to building Ukraine a sustainable long-term defence capability.
