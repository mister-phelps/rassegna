# Daily Briefing — Monday, 13 July 2026

## EU Affairs

**Coalition of the Willing convenes in Paris as allies face pressure to deliver on air defence**
Thirty-seven nations including Ukraine, NATO and the major European powers gathered at the Hôtel des Invalides in Paris this afternoon for a "Coalition of the Willing" summit, the largest gathering of the ad hoc grouping in months. According to the [Politico Brussels Playbook](https://politico.eu), the central question was how to meet Kyiv's urgent need for air defence at a moment when Russia continues to pound Ukrainian civilian infrastructure in some of the war's deadliest recent strikes. German Chancellor Friedrich Merz used the occasion to press Greece and Spain to hand over existing Patriot systems; Athens responded with studied caution, saying every request must be weighed against Greece's own "needs for its territorial defense." Trump has agreed to allow Ukraine to produce Patriot interceptor missiles under licence, but those are not immediately available. [Euractiv's Rapporteur](https://euractiv.com) notes that Macron is also pushing for closer European ballistic missile defence cooperation, including developing complementary anti-ballistic systems with significant Ukrainian industrial involvement. The Berlin Playbook notes Russian intelligence services have raised the probability of escalation against the West in the coming months, while Putin shows no willingness to negotiate and continues issuing maximalist demands.

**EU foreign ministers split on Israeli settlements trade ban**
The EU's Foreign Affairs Council met in Brussels with the settlements dispute centre stage. A group of member states — Belgium, the Netherlands and Spain among them — are pushing for an EU-wide ban on imports from illegal Israeli settlements, framing it as a matter of international law. According to the [Politico Brussels Playbook](https://politico.eu), Germany intends to abstain or veto any new trade proposals and the Czech Republic has rejected further sanctions; speaking privately, some EU officials argue that steep tariffs before Israel's October election would be counterproductive and could benefit hardline candidates such as National Security Minister Itamar Ben-Gvir. The European Commission presented a legal options paper to ministers but has not made a formal proposal that could be put to a vote — a deliberate delay, critics allege. [Euractiv](https://euractiv.com) frames the dispute as partly an institutional turf war between the Commission and Kaja Kallas's EEAS over foreign policy competence: Kallas has pressed Commission colleagues and accused them of "stymying" work on a legal review she requested in April. The dispute is "not whether the settlements are illegal — they are — but whether the Commission should require unanimity or a lower majority to prohibit trade." The next formal decision point is October.

**EU's 21st Russia sanctions package stalls; oil price cap deadline looms**
EU ambassadors failed to agree on the 21st Russia sanctions package at a special Coreper meeting on Sunday, and ministers were still seeking to close gaps at the Foreign Affairs Council on Monday. According to [Euractiv](https://euractiv.com), the remaining obstacles include a dispute over the transit of Russian LNG to non-EU countries and Austria's bid for an EU-level fix to Raiffeisen Bank's Russian operations; Greece objected to an 18-month delay to the LNG transit ban. The [Politico Brussels Playbook](https://politico.eu) reports that a deal on the oil price cap freeze — contested by Greece, Malta and Cyprus — may yet be struck, possibly shortened from six months to three as a compromise, but a proposed ban on former Russian combatants entering the EU remains unlikely given French and Italian objections. Plans to bar Patriarch Kirill have been dropped due to Bulgaria's refusal. If no package is agreed by Wednesday, the EU oil price cap will automatically rise on 15 July — an inadvertent benefit to Moscow.

**EU unveils €900 million "Team Gaza Initiative" amid UNRWA-Board of Peace rivalry**
The Palestine Donor Group convened in Brussels, where the Commission unveiled nearly €900 million in funding under a new "Team Gaza Initiative" for "early recovery actions" in Gaza, carefully avoiding the word "reconstruction." According to [Politico](https://politico.eu) and [Euractiv](https://euractiv.com), the initiative is designed as a complementary track to Trump's Board of Peace — with a different governance structure — while Brussels insists it is not a rival. UNRWA Commissioner-General Christian F. Saunders rejected what he called a "false narrative" about the agency and argued that UNRWA could help implement Trump's 20-point plan and "should be an integral part" of Gaza's recovery. [Euractiv](https://euractiv.com) notes that pledges made by Gulf states at the Board of Peace February meeting have yet to materialise, and that the EU has struggled to persuade Arab nations to contribute to its Palestinian initiatives.

## Italian Politics

**Italy pushes back on EU enlargement safeguards proposals**
Foreign Minister Antonio Tajani presented a non-paper in Brussels — co-signed by Austria, Croatia, Czechia, Greece, Slovakia and Slovenia, with Hungary attending for the first time — rejecting elements of a French, German and Benelux framework for future EU accession treaties. According to [Euractiv](https://euractiv.com), Italy argues that any new legal safeguards must be "proportionate, time-limited and linked to objectively identified needs" and must not create a "parallel regime" for new members. Rome opposes proposals that would withhold voting rights in sensitive policy areas or create an associate membership track for Ukraine. The counter-proposal comes ahead of an October European Council where leaders are expected to set the bloc's approach to enlargement.

## UK Politics

**Ann Widdecombe murder: suspect arrested as Farage draws criticism for speculation**
A 28-year-old white British man was arrested in Rotherham, South Yorkshire, on suspicion of murdering Ann Widdecombe, Reform UK spokesperson and former Tory minister. Devon and Cornwall police confirmed they are not looking for anyone else and state there is "nothing to suggest" the killing was politically motivated, with detectives remaining "open minded about any potential motive." According to [The Times](https://thetimes.com), the suspect is believed to have driven almost 300 miles to Widdecombe's Dartmoor home. Nigel Farage has drawn criticism — from opponents and from within Reform — for speculating publicly about the motive and for sifting through emails for abusive messages. As the [Politico Sunday Crunch](https://politico.eu) reports, former friend Harvey Proctor called Farage's statements "deeply disturbing," while Deputy Leader Richard Tice urged caution. The [Times](https://thetimes.com) notes that six in ten voters believe Farage acted incorrectly over a separate controversy — an undeclared £5 million crypto gift — with only half of Reform's own voters backing him.

**Andy Burnham set for Labour leadership; Reform leads in polls**
Andy Burnham faces questions from Labour MPs at a leadership hustings tonight — without any rivals — with a special conference on Friday expected to confirm him as party leader and prospective prime minister. According to the [Politico Sunday Crunch](https://politico.eu), his team is targeting early retail policies including intervention on energy bills and transport costs, and he is reportedly considering income tax cuts for lower earners. Burnham is also planning a summer tour to areas where Labour struggled under Starmer. Politico's Poll of Polls currently shows Reform UK at 25%, Labour at 21%, Conservatives at 19%.

## Foreign Policy

**Iran and the United States exchange heavy strikes; Strait of Hormuz declared closed**
The United States and Iran are engaged in their most serious military confrontation of the current crisis, with Washington striking Iran for the fifth time since last week in retaliation for attacks on commercial shipping in the Strait of Hormuz. According to [Le Grand Continent](https://legrandcontinent.eu/fr/2026/07/13/bataille-dormuz-la-guerre-entre-les-etats-unis-et-liran-a-repris/), the core dispute concerns competing interpretations of the May 17 memorandum of understanding: Washington insists Article V guarantees unrestricted freedom of navigation, while Tehran argues it retains the right to "coordinate and control" transit. Trump declared the MOU "dead" at the NATO summit, and has given the US military a year-long order to "decimate and destroy" Iranian territory. Iran's Revolutionary Guards struck a container ship and declared the Strait "closed until further notice"; new Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei has promised revenge. The [Reuters Iran Briefing](https://reuters.com) reports that US CENTCOM disputes Iranian claims, saying 140 ships have passed through the Strait in the past seven days. [Le Grand Continent](https://legrandcontinent.eu/fr/2026/07/13/bataille-dormuz-la-guerre-entre-les-etats-unis-et-liran-a-repris/) assesses that a return to an amended or restored agreement is likely within weeks, following further limited exchanges, barring US facilities being directly struck. The [Politico Brussels Playbook](https://politico.eu) notes that EU foreign ministers held a special lunch session with Gulf Cooperation Council counterparts to discuss the escalation, which loomed over all other agenda items.

**Death of Lindsey Graham raises concerns over Ukraine's transatlantic links**
Republican Senator Lindsey Graham died on Saturday at the age of 71 from a "brief and sudden illness," days after visiting Kyiv. As the [Politico Berlin Playbook](https://politico.eu) and [Reuters Daily Briefing](https://reuters.com) both note, Graham was among the most influential advocates for Ukraine in the US Senate and a trusted back-channel between Trump and European leaders. German MP Roderich Kiesewetter told Berlin Playbook that "Germany often leaned on him and his channels to Trump." Calls for an autopsy have emerged amid unconfirmed speculation about Russian involvement; FBI Director Kash Patel confirmed the Bureau is supporting local authorities.
