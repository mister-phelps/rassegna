# Daily Briefing — Monday, 20 July 2026

## EU Affairs

**Greece threatens to derail EU's 21st Russia sanctions package over LNG shipping**
The European Commission is scrambling to salvage a proposed ban on the transport of Russian liquefied natural gas from its 21st package of sanctions against Russia, according to four EU diplomats cited by the [Politico Brussels Playbook](https://www.politico.eu). Athens is withholding its approval — required under unanimity rules — to protect Dynagas, a company owned by billionaire shipowner George Prokopiou, which has transported some 30 million tonnes of Russian LNG worth an estimated $24 billion since the start of the war, per [Phastidio.net](https://phastidio.net/2026/07/20/ue-un-blocco-in-frantumi/). The EU executive is preparing an economic impact analysis ahead of a Wednesday meeting of EU ambassadors seen as make-or-break for the package. Other member states are also causing friction: Austria wants access to frozen Russian assets to compensate Raiffeisen Bank; Portugal and Germany are pushing to revoke bans on Russian fish imports; Italy and France want to ease visa restrictions on Russian military personnel. A derogation for Athens is possible as a last resort, though a second EU diplomat told Politico it would "send the wrong signal." As Phastidio's Mario Seminerio observes, the episode illustrates a broader pattern: EU collective action is eroding under the weight of divergent national economic interests.

**EU Parliament's €410 billion industrial fund deal collapses as EPP insists on scrapping green rules**
A deal in the European Parliament on the bloc's new €410 billion industrial fund broke down after the centre-right European People's Party insisted on removing green conditionalities from the legislation, three officials told [Politico's Morning Financial Services](https://www.politico.eu). The collapse represents a significant setback for EU industrial policy ambitions at a moment of intense competitive pressure.

**European Parliament to roll out US-made AI models despite tech-sovereignty push**
The European Parliament could deploy its internal "EPGenAI Hub" platform — giving MEPs and staff access to AI models from OpenAI, Anthropic, Meta and Mistral — as early as September, according to two people with knowledge of the matter cited by [Politico](https://www.politico.eu). The rollout sits uneasily with the Parliament's recent moves to reduce dependence on American technology, including its replacement of Google with French search engine Qwant. MEPs are not currently covered by the staff AI-use guidelines and face no disclosure requirements when using AI to draft amendments or speeches.

**Merz weighs cabinet reshuffle after Spahn resignation over surrogacy scandal**
German Chancellor Friedrich Merz told public television on Sunday that he is considering a government reshuffle after the resignation of CDU parliamentary group leader Jens Spahn, who stepped down following sharp criticism of his announcement that he and his husband had welcomed a child via a surrogate mother — a practice illegal in Germany and repeatedly opposed by Merz's own party, as [Politico](https://www.politico.eu) reports. The name of Thorsten Frei has been mentioned as a potential replacement. Politico's Berlin Playbook frames Spahn's exit as an opportunity for Merz to inject "fresh blood" into his government.

## UK Politics

**Andy Burnham becomes Britain's seventh prime minister in a decade**
Andy Burnham — the former Greater Manchester mayor dubbed "King of the North" — was sworn in as prime minister on Monday, promising to "rewire" the nation with a ten-year programme focused on cost-of-living pressures and underperforming public services, as [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com) and [The Times](https://www.thetimes.co.uk) report. [The Times](https://www.thetimes.co.uk) frames his accession as the completion of a "bloodless coup" within the Labour Party. The Trump administration has already signalled expectations, warning Burnham that Britain must make "sustained investment" in defence. Commentator Lynton Crosby writes in The Times that Starmer's successor will not fix the country unless Labour has understood what went wrong; Lord Kinnock is among those due to speak on Times Radio today on the challenges Burnham faces.

## Economy & Markets

**Brent crude tops $90 as Strait of Hormuz tensions intensify**
The Brent crude benchmark exceeded $90 per barrel for the first time in over a month on Monday morning, according to [Le Grand Continent](https://legrandcontinent.eu/fr/2026/07/20/les-etats-unis-se-preparent-ils-a-une-nouvelle-escalade-en-iran/), as markets priced in the risk of sustained disruption to Gulf shipping lanes. Iran's Revolutionary Guards claimed two tankers had "exploded" and been immobilised attempting to transit the Strait of Hormuz. The spike adds pressure across European economies already facing elevated energy costs.

**Ryanair profits fall by a third as Iran war dampens summer demand**
Ryanair reported a one-third slump in profits for the April–June quarter, citing higher fuel costs and weaker fares, as [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com) reports. The airline warned that summer fares look set to fall further amid consumer nervousness about the Iran conflict and the broader economic environment.

## Foreign Policy

**US-Iran war enters new phase as strikes hit nuclear sites and Hormuz shipping collapses**
The United States and Iran are locked in an intensifying cycle of strikes now entering its ninth consecutive day, with escalation indicators mounting on multiple fronts. According to [The Times](https://www.thetimes.co.uk) and [Le Grand Continent](https://legrandcontinent.eu/fr/2026/07/20/les-etats-unis-se-preparent-ils-a-une-nouvelle-escalade-en-iran/), the US struck the Darkhovin nuclear power plant under construction in Khuzestan province on Sunday — an attack Iran's state atomic agency called "a clear violation of international law." The UN's International Atomic Energy Agency noted the site contained no nuclear material when last visited and called for calm. Le Grand Continent further reports that on Monday morning, Iran's IRNA agency said the city of Bushehr — home to Iran's only operating nuclear plant — was also targeted, alongside strikes overnight in Khvormuj, Tabriz and Isfahan. Three US servicemen were killed over the weekend, bringing American fatalities to 18 since the start of the war. The US is deploying additional F-16s from Germany and F-35s from the United Kingdom to the Middle East, as [Le Grand Continent](https://legrandcontinent.eu/fr/2026/07/20/les-etats-unis-se-preparent-ils-a-une-nouvelle-escalade-en-iran/) reports, and has sent dozens of additional refuelling aircraft to Israel. President Trump declared Sunday that Iran has "lost almost everything militarily." [Politico's Playbook](https://www.politico.eu) flags that the IRGC also struck US military assets across the region, "all but wrecking" the interim ceasefire memorandum signed last month. The [Reuters Iran Briefing](https://www.reuters.com) notes that few tankers are entering Hormuz to load oil, with LNG floating storage rising — a sign of sustained supply disruption.

**Hungary: opposition nominates chess legend Judit Polgár for presidency**
Opposition leader Péter Magyar announced on Sunday that he would nominate Judit Polgár — widely regarded as the greatest female chess player in history — to serve as Hungary's new head of state, following President Tamás Sulyok's effective removal from office via a constitutional amendment he signed himself, according to [Politico](https://www.politico.eu) and [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com).
