# Daily Briefing — Sunday, 19 July 2026

## EU Affairs

**Washington launches funding programme for MAGA-aligned organisations in Europe**
The US State Department's Bureau of Democracy, Human Rights and Labour launched on 13 July a call for proposals to develop "civilizational bonds, democratic resilience, and rule of law in Europe", with a budget of nearly $5 million available to NGOs, think tanks, universities and individuals — grants running up to $3 million each. According to [Le Grand Continent](https://legrandcontinent.eu/fr/2026/07/17/offensive-maga-contre-leurope-les-etats-unis-lancent-un-programme-de-financement-dorganisations-alignees/), this is the first time since 2025 that the Trump administration has sought to carry its ideological agenda in Europe through direct financial support, with analyst Maya Kandel describing the bureau as having become "the instrument and the funder of civil society actors mobilised, in allied European countries, against their own institutions." German Chancellor Friedrich Merz publicly warned Washington on 15 July against any attempt to interfere in German elections, and Elon Musk compounded tensions a day later by posting on X that Marine Le Pen was "France's last hope." Le Grand Continent notes that the programme sits alongside a parallel US diplomatic offensive: Secretary of State Marco Rubio convened a meeting on 16 July on "global far-left terrorism", with Washington describing Europe as an "incubator of terrorist threats" — what Kandel calls "two prongs of the same enterprise of ideological vassalisation."

**China more favourably viewed than the United States for the first time in 20 years**
A Pew Research Center survey conducted between February and May across 36 countries finds that 27 of them now hold a more favourable view of China than of the United States, including France, Germany, Italy, and Spain. As [Le Grand Continent](https://legrandcontinent.eu/fr/2026/07/18/effet-trump-pour-la-premiere-fois-depuis-20-ans-la-chine-est-percue-plus-positivement-dans-le-monde-que-les-etats-unis/) reports, an average of 46% of respondents view Beijing favourably — up 14 points since 2023 — against just 36% for Washington, down 22 points. Three-quarters of respondents consider the US to have a tendency to interfere in other countries' affairs, compared with 45% for China. In Europe, only Poland and Hungary retain a net positive view of the United States. Even within the US, 27% of Americans now hold a favourable opinion of China, the highest since 2019. The findings directly contradict Trump's claim in a 17 July address to the nation that "today we are the most prominent country in the world."

## Economy & Markets

**Russian stock market at 2022 lows as oligarchs move billions abroad**
The Moscow Exchange (MOEX) index fell to 2,022 points on 16 July — back to the trough reached immediately after the 2022 invasion of Ukraine — having lost 27% of its value since the start of 2026. As [Le Grand Continent](https://legrandcontinent.eu/fr/2026/07/18/economie-russe-lindice-de-la-bourse-de-moscou-a-chute-de-27-depuis-le-debut-de-lannee/) reports, the decline reflects the cumulative effect of Western sanctions and, crucially, Ukrainian drone strikes that have progressively paralysed Russian oil infrastructure. The degradation of Russia's economic situation is now worrying even those in circles close to Vladimir Putin: according to Bloomberg (16 July), several of Russia's wealthiest individuals — some with ties to the Kremlin — have transferred billions of dollars abroad since the beginning of the year, redirecting their portfolios into cryptocurrencies, gold, foreign real estate, and private equity funds, particularly in Gulf states. Russian oligarchs fear asset seizure following Communist Party leader Gennady Zyuganov's end-of-June suggestion that 30 trillion roubles of private savings could be "mobilised" to cover the budget deficit — a comment he subsequently attempted to walk back.

## Foreign Policy

**US renews strikes on Iran after ceasefire collapses**
The United States renewed military strikes on Iran after two American military personnel were killed in an Iranian attack, according to a [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com) Iran Briefing (19 July). The strikes mark a sharp escalation: an interim ceasefire deal signed a month ago fell apart last week, and the two sides have since intensified their exchange of attacks, raising what Reuters describes as "the possibility of a return to all-out war." Iran's Supreme Leader declared that American breaches of the agreement show that Trump's signature is "worthless." Separately, Lebanese President Aoun is reported to be heading to the White House in hopes of generating pressure on Israel, while Israeli strikes killed Palestinians attending a funeral in Gaza for victims of an earlier attack. The Phastidio.net weekly digest of 18 July notes that Trump is "increasingly grotesquely entangled" on the Strait of Hormuz and is being "trolled by Iranians", and warns that the oil supply clock is running down — with potential energy-price consequences for Italy and Europe.

**Ukraine's political crisis: Zelensky dismisses drone war architect Fedorov**
President Volodymyr Zelensky dismissed Defence Minister Mykhailo Fedorov on 15 July following his return from the Bastille Day parade in Paris, triggering what [Le Grand Continent](https://legrandcontinent.eu/fr/2026/07/17/en-ukraine-le-limogeage-du-ministre-de-la-defense-fedorov-pourrait-avoir-des-consequences-sur-la-trajectoire-de-la-guerre/) describes as the most serious political crisis in Ukraine since the replacement of General Zaloujny in 2024. Fedorov — the former digital transformation minister who had turned the Defence Ministry into a centre of drone warfare innovation — revealed at a press conference on 16 July that he had asked Zelensky to remove Commander-in-Chief Oleksandr Syrsky, a request the president declined. Protests broke out in at least 19 Ukrainian cities; several military officers resigned, including Colonel Pavlo Yelizarov, deputy commander of the air force, who called the decision "a severe blow to the country's defence." Analyst Tatarigami, a former officer with considerable influence in military circles, said that "President Zelensky preferred the comfort of the incompetent old guard over change and a new vision." The dismissal is widely read as a victory for Syrsky's network over the technology-driven reformers, and risks slowing the drone modernisation programme that has helped Ukraine blunt Russian advances in recent months.
